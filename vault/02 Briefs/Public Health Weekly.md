# Public Health Weekly

> fleet role evolves this single note in place each cycle. azimuth reports **recorded disease
> outbreak notifications** — the disease, the place, the case count and the alert level as the
> reporting body published them — never a health prediction, never advice. Every claim links
> to the L1 note it rests on. (First cycle, written from the 2026-07-15 ingest.)

## This week at a glance

- The WHO / CDC outbreak channel carries **173 active outbreak notifications** as of the
  2026-07-22 pull — down from 184 on 2026-07-20 and stable at 173 across both the 07-21 and 07-22
  pulls. By the feed's own alert bands: **128 watch** and **45 alert**; no warning-level entry is
  carried — the warning-level **measles outbreak in North Darfur's El Taweisha district (Sudan)**
  has stayed off the feed since the 07-20 pull ([[disease-outbreaks]]).
- **Measles leads the slate at 86 of 173 notifications** — ahead of unknown disease (24),
  polio (18), hepatitis A (8), diphtheria (8) and whooping cough (4); a pair of **Ebola**
  notifications newly appear on the slate this cycle while an earlier H5N1 pair drops off, and the
  feed's "unknown disease" label still covers 24 entries pending identification
  ([[disease-outbreaks]]).
- The largest single alert-level outbreak on the feed remains **measles in Bangladesh**, which
  the 2026-07-22 pull records at **120,000 suspected and confirmed cases**. Other major measles
  entries include **Guatemala City (~21,700 cases, alert)**, **Mongolia (~15,475 cases, watch)**
  and **Mexico (~11,771 cases, watch)** ([[disease-outbreaks]]).
- Alert-level (non-watch) notifications number **45 of the 173** on the 07-22 pull — led by
  measles entries, with the US carrying the largest single-country cluster of notifications (21) —
  and the remaining **128 at watch level**; azimuth reports each notification in the alert band the
  reporting body assigned it ([[disease-outbreaks]]).
