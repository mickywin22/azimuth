# Climate Signals Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`
> fleet role evolves this single note in place each week. azimuth reports **observed**
> climate measurements — recorded atmospheric concentrations, recorded sea-ice extent, and
> recorded regional temperature/precipitation anomalies — never a forecast of what the climate
> will do. Every claim links to the L1 note it rests on. This brief reads three independent
> instrument channels at once (atmospheric chemistry, the cryosphere, regional reanalysis) for
> the seven days to 2026-06-24, the synthesis a static single-source bundle cannot do.

## This week at a glance

- Three independent channels point the same direction this cycle: atmospheric CO2 sits at a
  recorded 430.78 ppm — about 54% above the pre-industrial 280 ppm baseline — Arctic sea-ice
  extent is 1.28 million km² below its long-term average, and 14 of the 22 monitored land/ocean
  zones ran warm over the week ([[co2-monitoring]], [[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
- The single most extreme regional anomaly recorded this week was the Western Antarctic Ice
  Sheet at +6.2 °C above normal (flagged `EXTREME`) — a Southern-Hemisphere winter polar-warm
  signal far larger than any other zone in the feed ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The Arctic shows up in two channels at once — recorded sea-ice extent of 10.11 million km²
  (1.28 below average, the feed's "below_average" flag) alongside a +1.8 °C warm-air anomaly
  over the same region — a cross-channel join: low recorded ice co-located with a recorded
  warm-air anomaly ([[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).

## Atmospheric chemistry (Mauna Loa)

- The NOAA Mauna Loa record reads a current 430.78 ppm CO2, 1.45 ppm above the year-ago 429.33
  ppm, with the feed's stated annual growth rate at 2.86 ppm/yr; the May monthly average of
  432.34 ppm marks the usual Northern-Hemisphere spring seasonal peak ([[co2-monitoring]]).
- The same station reads two other long-lived greenhouse gases this cycle: methane at 1940.46
  ppb and nitrous oxide at 339.75 ppb — recorded concentrations reported as-measured, with no
  projection attached ([[co2-monitoring]]).
- The 12-month trend in the feed runs from 429.61 ppm (2025-06) through a seasonal dip to
  424.37 ppm (2025-09) and back up to 432.34 ppm (2026-05), the saw-tooth seasonal cycle riding
  on the rising baseline that the year-on-year gap measures ([[co2-monitoring]]).

## Cryosphere & ocean (sea-ice channel)

- Arctic sea-ice extent is recorded at 10.11 million km² for 2026-06, an anomaly of −1.28
  million km² and flagged "below_average"; every one of the last twelve monthly readings in the
  feed carries a negative anomaly (from −1.13 to −2.19 million km²) ([[sea-ice-extent]]).
- The same ocean channel reports a global sea-surface-temperature anomaly of +0.61 °C, mean sea
  level 97.8 mm above the 1993 reference at a recorded 4.4 mm/yr rise, and upper-ocean
  (0–700 m) heat content of 228.45 zettajoules — four recorded ocean measurements in one pull
  ([[sea-ice-extent]]).

## Regional anomaly map (22 zones)

- Of the 22 monitored zones for the week to 2026-06-24, 14 were flagged warm, 5 cold, 2 dry and
  1 wet — a warm-leaning week, with the Middle East (+3.0 °C) and Central Asia (+3.0 °C) the
  warmest non-polar zones (both `MODERATE` severity) ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The driest signals were the Coral Triangle (precip anomaly −7.1) and the Taiwan Strait
  (−6.5), both `MODERATE`; the lone wet outlier was Myanmar (+6.7, `MODERATE`); the coldest
  zones were Australia (−2.2 °C), the Sahel (−1.3 °C) and Greenland (−1.2 °C)
  ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- Warm anomalies clustered across the Mediterranean (+2.4 °C), Ukraine (+2.6 °C), the Congo
  Basin (+2.5 °C), the Tibetan Plateau (+2.0 °C) and California (+2.0 °C), while the Western
  Antarctic Ice Sheet's +6.2 °C `EXTREME` reading stood apart from the rest of the field
  ([[climate-anomalies]]).

## Reading the week

- Read together, the three channels describe a consistent recorded picture: a record-high CO2
  concentration, a sea-ice extent that has stayed below its average for twelve straight months,
  and a warm-leaning regional map with both poles registering warm anomalies. azimuth reports
  what the instruments measured — the value, the anomaly sign, the severity flag — and stops
  there; the editorial line keeps the brief to observed facts and excludes any projection of
  what follows ([[co2-monitoring]], [[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
- The synthesis value this cycle is the cross-channel join a single feed cannot make: the
  Arctic's low recorded ice extent and its recorded warm-air anomaly are two separate
  measurements that line up, and the polar pair (Arctic +1.8 °C, Western Antarctic +6.2 °C)
  both register warm in the same window ([[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
