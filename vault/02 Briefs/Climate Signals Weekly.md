# Climate Signals Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`
> fleet role evolves this single note in place each week. azimuth reports **observed**
> climate measurements — recorded atmospheric concentrations, recorded sea-ice extent, and
> recorded regional temperature/precipitation anomalies — never a forecast of what the climate
> will do. Every claim links to the L1 note it rests on. This brief reads three independent
> instrument channels at once (atmospheric chemistry, the cryosphere, regional reanalysis) for
> the seven days to 2026-07-13, the synthesis a static single-source bundle cannot do.

## This week at a glance

- The cryosphere eased off last cycle's headline: the NSIDC feed's Arctic trend flag retreated
  from **"record_low"** back to **"below_average"** — recorded July extent of 8.26 million km² at
  a −1.63 million km² anomaly (a shallower deficit than the prior −1.8, the summer melt lowering
  the raw extent) — while atmospheric CO2 reads a monthly-average 431.44 ppm (~54% above the
  pre-industrial 280 ppm baseline) and 17 of the 25 monitored land/ocean zones ran warm over the
  week ([[sea-ice-extent]], [[co2-monitoring]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
- The regional map's heat extreme cleared: **no zone** holds the `EXTREME` warm band this cycle —
  Europe eased to **+3 °C** `MODERATE` (from +6.1 °C) and Central Asia is the warmest zone at
  **+4 °C** `MODERATE` — while the two `EXTREME` flags are now the Western Antarctic Ice Sheet
  (−9.5 °C cold) and a Myanmar monsoon wet signal (precip anomaly +13.7) ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The Arctic cross-channel join held its sign — below-average recorded ice extent (8.26 million
  km²) alongside a +1.4 °C warm-air anomaly over the same region — while the Antarctic pole read
  an `EXTREME` −9.5 °C cold, the two poles registering with opposite signs ([[sea-ice-extent]],
  [[climate-anomalies]]).

## Atmospheric chemistry (Mauna Loa)

- The NOAA Mauna Loa record reads a current 428.42 ppm CO2 (measured 2026-07-10), 0.55 ppm above
  the year-ago 427.87 ppm, with the feed's stated annual growth rate at 2.85 ppm/yr; the June
  monthly average of 431.44 ppm sits above the current daily reading as the Northern-Hemisphere
  summer seasonal draw-down pulls the daily value below the spring peak ([[co2-monitoring]]).
- The same station reads two other long-lived greenhouse gases this cycle: methane at 1937.86
  ppb (easing from 1940.46) and nitrous oxide at 339.76 ppb — recorded concentrations reported
  as-measured, with no projection attached ([[co2-monitoring]]).
- The 12-month trend in the feed runs from 427.87 ppm (2025-07) through a seasonal dip to
  424.37 ppm (2025-09) and back up to the 431.44 ppm June average, the saw-tooth seasonal cycle
  riding on the rising baseline that the year-on-year gap measures ([[co2-monitoring]]).

## Cryosphere & ocean (sea-ice channel)

- Arctic sea-ice extent is recorded at 8.26 million km² for 2026-07, an anomaly of −1.63
  million km² — and the feed's trend flag retreated to **"below_average"** this cycle, down a
  notch from the "record_low" it carried before; the deficit eased from the prior pull's −1.8
  even as the raw extent fell on the summer melt, and every one of the last twelve monthly
  readings in the feed still carries a negative anomaly, a twelve-month unbroken run below average
  ([[sea-ice-extent]]).
- The same ocean channel reports a global sea-surface-temperature anomaly of +0.61 °C (held), mean
  sea level 98.7 mm above the 1993 reference at a recorded 4.4 mm/yr rise (up from 98.1 mm), and
  upper-ocean (0–700 m) heat content of 228.45 zettajoules — four recorded ocean measurements in
  one pull ([[sea-ice-extent]]).

## Regional anomaly map (25 zones)

- Of the 25 monitored zones for the week 2026-07-07 to 2026-07-13, 17 were flagged warm, 7 cold
  and 1 wet — still warm-leaning — and the top of the warm field eased out of `EXTREME`: Central
  Asia (+4 °C) and Europe (+3 °C) lead as `MODERATE` warm zones, with no zone reaching the
  `EXTREME` warm band this cycle (Europe down from +6.1 °C) ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The two `EXTREME` flags this cycle are not heat: the Western Antarctic Ice Sheet at −9.5 °C
  cold (sharply deeper than the prior −0.8 °C) and Myanmar carrying an `EXTREME` wet signal
  (precip anomaly +13.7) as the only zone flagged wet; the coldest reading is that Antarctic
  −9.5 °C, ahead of the North Atlantic (−1.0 °C) ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- Greenland ran warm at +2.5 °C and the Congo Basin at +2.9 °C, while the Arctic zone held a
  +1.4 °C warm-air anomaly over the below-average ice — the bipolar split of recent cycles
  hardened, the Antarctic pole now an `EXTREME` cold against the warm Arctic air
  ([[climate-anomalies]]).

  ~54% above pre-industrial and 0.55 ppm above a year ago, a sea-ice extent whose twelve-month
  below-average run held even as the trend flag retreated from "record_low" to "below_average" on
  the summer melt, and a regional map that stayed warm-leaning (17 of 25 zones) while its warm top
  eased out of `EXTREME` entirely. azimuth reports what the instruments measured — the value, the
  anomaly sign, the severity flag — and stops there; the editorial line keeps the brief to
  observed facts and excludes any projection of what follows ([[co2-monitoring]],

- The synthesis value this cycle is the cross-channel join a single feed cannot make: the
  Arctic's below-average recorded ice extent (8.26 million km²) sits alongside a +1.4 °C warm-air
  anomaly over the same region, while the Antarctic pole recorded an `EXTREME` −9.5 °C cold — the
  poles continue to register with opposite signs, and the Antarctic cold sharpened this cycle
  ([[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
