# Climate Signals Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`
> fleet role evolves this single note in place each week. azimuth reports **observed**
> climate measurements — recorded atmospheric concentrations, recorded sea-ice extent, and
> recorded regional temperature/precipitation anomalies — never a forecast of what the climate
> will do. Every claim links to the L1 note it rests on. This brief reads three independent
> instrument channels at once (atmospheric chemistry, the cryosphere, regional reanalysis) for
> the seven days to 2026-07-02, the synthesis a static single-source bundle cannot do.

## This week at a glance

- The cryosphere carried this cycle's headline: the NSIDC feed's Arctic trend flag flipped from
  "below_average" to **"record_low"** — recorded June extent of 9.1 million km² at a −1.8
  million km² anomaly, the deepest deficit the feed has carried since the brief began — while
  atmospheric CO2 reads 430.48 ppm (~54% above the pre-industrial 280 ppm baseline) and 16 of
  the 25 monitored land/ocean zones ran warm over the week ([[sea-ice-extent]],
  [[co2-monitoring]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
- The regional map eased at the top: **Europe alone** holds the `EXTREME` band at **+6.1 °C**
  above normal (down from +9.9 °C), while Ukraine eased out of `EXTREME` to a +3.8 °C
  `MODERATE` reading; the dry signal moved to East Asia (precip anomaly −7.0, `MODERATE`)
  ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The Arctic cross-channel join softened but held its sign — record-low recorded ice extent
  (9.1 million km²) alongside a now-mild +0.7 °C warm-air anomaly over the same region (from
  +2.8 °C) — low recorded ice still co-located with a recorded warm-air reading
  ([[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).

## Atmospheric chemistry (Mauna Loa)

- The NOAA Mauna Loa record reads a current 430.48 ppm CO2, 1.49 ppm above the year-ago 428.99
  ppm, with the feed's stated annual growth rate at 2.86 ppm/yr; the May monthly average of
  432.34 ppm marks the usual Northern-Hemisphere spring seasonal peak, so the current daily
  reading sits just below it as the seasonal draw-down continues ([[co2-monitoring]]).
- The same station reads two other long-lived greenhouse gases this cycle: methane at 1940.46
  ppb and nitrous oxide at 339.75 ppb — recorded concentrations reported as-measured, with no
  projection attached ([[co2-monitoring]]).
- The 12-month trend in the feed runs from 429.61 ppm (2025-06) through a seasonal dip to
  424.37 ppm (2025-09) and back up to 432.34 ppm (2026-05), the saw-tooth seasonal cycle riding
  on the rising baseline that the year-on-year gap measures ([[co2-monitoring]]).

## Cryosphere & ocean (sea-ice channel)

- Arctic sea-ice extent is recorded at 9.1 million km² for 2026-06, an anomaly of −1.8
  million km² — and the feed's trend flag reads **"record_low"** this cycle, up a notch from
  the "below_average" it carried before; the deficit deepened again from the prior pull's
  −1.61, and every one of the last twelve monthly readings in the feed carries a negative
  anomaly, a twelve-month unbroken run below average ([[sea-ice-extent]]).
- The same ocean channel reports a global sea-surface-temperature anomaly of +0.61 °C, mean sea
  level 98.1 mm above the 1993 reference at a recorded 4.4 mm/yr rise, and upper-ocean
  (0–700 m) heat content of 228.45 zettajoules — four recorded ocean measurements in one pull
  ([[sea-ice-extent]]).

## Regional anomaly map (25 zones)

- Of the 25 monitored zones for the week 2026-06-26 to 2026-07-02, 16 were flagged warm, 8 cold
  and 1 dry — still warm-leaning but less lopsided than the prior 19/5/1 — and the top of the
  field eased: Europe alone holds `EXTREME` at +6.1 °C (from +9.9 °C), ahead of the `MODERATE`
  warm zones Ukraine (+3.8 °C) and the Congo Basin (+3.7 °C) ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The dry signal migrated: East Asia is this week's lone dry zone (precip anomaly −7.0,
  `MODERATE`) while the Coral Triangle — last cycle's dry flag at the same −7.0 — flipped to a
  +1.4 °C warm reading; the coldest zone is now California at −3.8 °C (`MODERATE`), ahead of
  Greenland (−2.1 °C) and the North Atlantic (−2.0 °C) ([[climate-anomalies]]).
- The Western Antarctic Ice Sheet stayed on the cold side at −0.8 °C (easing from −2.2 °C) —
  the bipolar warm join of earlier cycles remains broken — and South Asia flipped back to warm
  (+2.5 °C, from −1.7 °C cold last cycle) ([[climate-anomalies]]).

## Reading the week

- Read together, the three channels describe a consistent recorded picture: a CO2 concentration
  ~54% above pre-industrial and 1.5 ppm above a year ago, a sea-ice extent whose twelve-month
  below-average run deepened into the feed's first **"record_low"** flag, and a regional map
  that stayed warm-leaning (16 of 25 zones) while its top eased to a single `EXTREME` anomaly
  over Europe. azimuth reports what the instruments measured — the value, the anomaly sign, the
  severity flag — and stops there; the editorial line keeps the brief to observed facts and
  excludes any projection of what follows ([[co2-monitoring]], [[sea-ice-extent]],
  [[climate-anomalies]]).
- The synthesis value this cycle is the cross-channel join a single feed cannot make: the
  Arctic's record-low recorded ice extent (9.1 million km²) sits alongside a still-positive
  (if mild, +0.7 °C) warm-air anomaly over the same region, while the Antarctic pole held its
  cold reading — the poles continue to register with opposite signs, unlike the warm-in-tandem
  picture of two cycles ago ([[sea-ice-extent]], [[climate-anomalies]]).
