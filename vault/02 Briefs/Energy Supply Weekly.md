## This week at a glance

- European gas supply is building comfortably: storage reached 2,686 Bcf as of 2026-06-05,
  a +108 Bcf weekly build and the eighth straight weekly gain of the refill season
  ([[natural-gas-storage-eu]]).
- The oil side is tightening: US crude inventories drew down ~15.2 million barrels on the
  week even as both crude benchmarks firmed into the high-$90s ([[crude-oil-inventories]],
  [[energy-prices]]).
- At the pump the picture is mixed — German diesel ticked up while most EU petrol markets
  eased week-on-week ([[fuel-prices]]).

## Storage and inventories

- EU gas storage stood at 2,686 Bcf for the week ending 2026-06-05, up 108 Bcf on the prior
  week (2,578 Bcf) and up from 2,063 Bcf eight weeks earlier — a steady, uninterrupted
  pre-summer refill ([[natural-gas-storage-eu]]).
- US commercial crude inventories fell to 775.7 million barrels for the week ending
  2026-06-05, a draw of ~15.2 million barrels, continuing an eight-week run of weekly draws
  down from ~870.8 million barrels in mid-April ([[crude-oil-inventories]]).
- The two stockpiles point in opposite directions this week: gas keeps building while crude
  keeps drawing — a comfortable gas balance alongside a tightening oil balance
  ([[natural-gas-storage-eu]], [[crude-oil-inventories]]).

## Prices

- Both crude benchmarks firmed: WTI was quoted at $96.87/barrel and Brent at $98.95/barrel,
  each higher on the latest reading, consistent with the ongoing US inventory draw
  ([[energy-prices]], [[crude-oil-inventories]]).
- German road fuel was steady-to-firmer: diesel averaged €1.87/L (+1.0% week-on-week) while
  E5 petrol eased slightly to €1.944/L (−0.4%) ([[fuel-prices]]).
- The wider European pump market leaned softer on petrol, with most reporting countries
  showing week-on-week declines, while US pump prices fell more sharply (diesel −2.6%,
  gasoline −3.7%) ([[fuel-prices]]).

## Reading the week

- The supply signals diverge by fuel: gas storage comfort versus a multi-week crude draw and
  firmer crude prices. The data describes a well-supplied gas market and a gradually
  tightening oil market, with retail fuel costs still broadly flat ([[natural-gas-storage-eu]],
  [[crude-oil-inventories]], [[energy-prices]], [[fuel-prices]]).
