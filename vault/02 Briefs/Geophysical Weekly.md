# Geophysical Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`
> fleet role evolves this single note in place each week. azimuth reports **observed**
> seismicity from the USGS feed — what was recorded, never what will happen. Every claim
> links to the L1 note it rests on. (This cycle reads the live `2026-06-20` ingest, covering
> the seven days to 2026-06-20.)

## This week at a glance

- The USGS feed recorded 110 magnitude-4.5-or-greater earthquakes worldwide over the seven
  days to 2026-06-20, of which 40 were M5+ and 6 reached M6+ — more strong events than the
  prior week (34 M5+, 4 M6+) ([[earthquakes]]).
- The largest single event was again an M6.7, 43 km ESE of Palu, Indonesia, part of a dense
  multi-day cluster of M5–M6 events around Palu and the wider Sulawesi region ([[earthquakes]]).
- Activity stayed concentrated along the western Pacific subduction arcs — the Philippines (22
  events), Indonesia (16) and Japan (9) led the count — but the week's new feature was a
  Kamchatka sequence east-southeast of Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, Russia, alongside the
  continuing China cluster south of Dunhuang ([[earthquakes]]).

## Largest events

- The week's strongest was M6.7 near Palu, Indonesia, followed by twin M6.6 events — one
  east-southeast of Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, Russia and one on the central Mid-Atlantic
  Ridge — an M6.3 south-southeast of Dunhuang, China, an M6.2 east-southeast of Pondaguitan in
  the southern Philippines, and an M6.0 off Kamchatka — six M6+ events in seven days
  ([[earthquakes]]).
- The Kamchatka pair (M6.6 then M6.0, both within an hour and ~130–160 km ESE of
  Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky) is the clearest change from last week, when no M6 sat off
  Kamchatka; a shallow M5.5 near Noda, Japan also registered on the northwest-Pacific margin
  ([[earthquakes]]).

## Where the activity clustered

- The southern Philippines (Sarangani–Balangonan–Pondaguitan) again produced the densest
  single sequence of the week, with dozens of M4.5–M5.5 events — a sustained aftershock-style
  swarm rather than isolated shocks ([[earthquakes]]).
- A second prominent cluster sat around Palu / Sulawesi, Indonesia, including the week's
  largest event; a third ran south of Dunhuang, China (peaking at M6.3); and a new fourth
  cluster appeared off Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, Russia, with seven events to M6.6
  ([[earthquakes]]).

## Depth profile

- Most events were shallow (≈10 km), but the feed also recorded deep-focus earthquakes,
  the deepest a 606 km event in the Fiji region — the deep Tonga–Fiji slab continues to
  register activity well below the crust ([[earthquakes]]).

## Reading the week

- The week's pattern remains typical for the western Pacific "Ring of Fire": high event counts
  concentrated on the Philippine and Indonesian subduction arcs, and no recorded shock above
  M7. What shifted from last week is the count of strong events — six M6+ against four, with a
  fresh Kamchatka sequence adding to the standing Philippine, Sulawesi and Dunhuang clusters.
  The data describes elevated but ordinary background seismicity along the world's most active
  plate boundaries ([[earthquakes]]).
