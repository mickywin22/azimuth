# Geophysical Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`

> seismicity from the USGS feed — what was recorded, never what will happen. Every claim

## This week at a glance

- The USGS feed recorded 101 magnitude-4.5-or-greater earthquakes worldwide over the seven
  days to 2026-07-10, of which 38 were M5+, **none reached M6+ and none M7+** — the strong end
  stepped down another notch as the prior cycle's four M6 events (the M6.5 Sarangani, the deep
  M6.1 Jurm and the two M6.0s off Noda and El Progreso) all aged out of the rolling window
  ([[earthquakes]]).
- The strongest events are now a set of **four M5.8 shocks** spread across three plate settings:
  91 km SSW of Isangel, **Vanuatu** (10 km), 296 km SSE of Ushuaia, **Argentina** (10 km, on the
  Scotia margin), 48 km SSW of Sarangani, **Philippines** (35 km) and a deep one 274 km SE of
  Levuka, **Fiji** (686 km) ([[earthquakes]]).
- The week's texture was sequences and swarms rather than great shocks: a dense Mindanao cluster
  around Sarangani/Balangonan, a **new Reykjanes Ridge swarm** off Iceland, an inland **Sichuan
  (China)** sequence and a central-Chile margin string ([[earthquakes]]).

## Largest events

- No M6+ event fell in the seven-day window for the first time in several cycles; the strongest
  are four M5.8 shocks — 91 km SSW of Isangel, Vanuatu (10 km), 296 km SSE of Ushuaia, Argentina
  (10 km), 48 km SSW of Sarangani, Philippines (35 km) and the deep 274 km SE of Levuka, Fiji
  (686 km). The prior cycle's M6.5 (Sarangani), M6.1 (Jurm) and two M6.0s (Noda, El Progreso)
  have all rolled out of the window, taking the M6+ count from four to zero ([[earthquakes]]).
- The four strongest span an oceanic-arc setting (Vanuatu), the far-southern Scotia/Drake margin
  (off Ushuaia), a subduction margin (Sarangani, Mindanao) and a deep-slab focus (Levuka, Fiji at
  686 km) — a broad, moderate strong-event picture with no single great shock and no arc
  dominating the top ([[earthquakes]]).

## Where the activity clustered

- Indonesia and the Philippines led the count at 12 events each, followed by China (9), Japan and
  a new Reykjanes Ridge swarm (6 each) and Chile (6). The Philippine tally concentrated on the
  Mindanao margin — the Sarangani cluster (M5.8, M5.4 and three M4.9s) plus the Balangonan string
  (M5.2, two M4.8s, an M4.5) — while Indonesia's fell across the Molucca Sea and Halmahera
  (Tobelo, Ternate, Bitung, Abepura) ([[earthquakes]]).
- Two continental / mid-ocean features stood out from the usual subduction arcs: an inland
  **Sichuan, China** sequence (Xunchang M5.0, Changning M5.1, Mianzhu/Deyang three M5.0s,
  Pianjiao) — intraplate activity away from a plate boundary — and a fresh **Reykjanes Ridge**
  swarm on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge off Iceland (six M4.9–M5.3 shocks, all ~10 km shallow). A
  central-Chile margin string (La Ligua M5.1, Coquimbo two M5.0s, Ovalle) and the far-southern
  Balleny Islands / Scotia arc rounded out the week ([[earthquakes]]).

## Depth profile

- Nearly half the window was shallow (50 events at ≤15 km, including the Reykjanes Ridge swarm,
  the Ushuaia M5.8 and much of the Chile string), but the deep-focus list stayed anchored on the
  Fiji slab: four events below 500 km — the deepest and strongest a single M5.8 at 686 km SE of
  Levuka, joined by M4.5 foci at 624 km, 576 km and 532 km in the Fiji region — alongside
  intermediate-depth shocks under Vanuatu (160 km), Lae, Papua New Guinea (183 km) and a 388 km
  focus SE of Kumano, Japan; the Tonga–Fiji slab continues to register activity well below the
  crust ([[earthquakes]]).

## Reading the week

- The count held typical for the world's active plate boundaries (101 M4.5+, in line with the
  prior 100; 38 M5+, in line with 37) — Indonesia, the Philippines and China leading — while the
  strong-event picture stepped down further: with the prior cycle's four M6 events aged out and
  no new M6 recorded, the window's ceiling fell to four M5.8 shocks. The week's character is
  sequences and swarms — the Mindanao Sarangani/Balangonan cluster, a new Reykjanes Ridge swarm,
  an inland Sichuan sequence and a central-Chile string — rather than any single great shock.
  azimuth records what the instruments measured — magnitude, location, depth, time — and stops
  there ([[earthquakes]]).
