# Geophysical Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`
> fleet role evolves this single note in place each week. azimuth reports **observed**
> seismicity from the USGS feed — what was recorded, never what will happen. Every claim
> links to the L1 note it rests on. (This cycle reads the live `2026-06-18` ingest, covering
> the seven days to 2026-06-18.)

## This week at a glance

- The USGS feed recorded 111 magnitude-4.5-or-greater earthquakes worldwide over the seven
  days to 2026-06-18, of which 34 were M5+ and 4 reached M6+ ([[earthquakes]]).
- The largest single event was an M6.7, 43 km ESE of Palu, Indonesia, part of a dense
  multi-day cluster of M5–M6 events around Palu and the wider Sulawesi region ([[earthquakes]]).
- Activity was concentrated along the western Pacific subduction arcs: the Philippines (27
  events), Indonesia (15) and Japan led the count, with a secondary China cluster south of
  Dunhuang ([[earthquakes]]).

## Largest events

- The week's strongest was M6.7 near Palu, Indonesia, followed by an M6.6 on the central
  Mid-Atlantic Ridge, an M6.3 south-southeast of Dunhuang, China, and an M6.2 east-southeast
  of Pondaguitan in the southern Philippines — four M6+ events in seven days ([[earthquakes]]).
- A shallow M5.5 struck near Noda, Japan, and an M5.7 was recorded at depth east-southeast of
  Ol'ga, Russia; both sit within the active northwest-Pacific margin ([[earthquakes]]).

## Where the activity clustered

- The southern Philippines (Sarangani–Balangonan–Pondaguitan) produced the densest single
  sequence of the week, with dozens of M4.5–M5.5 events — a sustained aftershock-style swarm
  rather than isolated shocks ([[earthquakes]]).
- A second prominent cluster sat around Palu / Sulawesi, Indonesia, including the week's
  largest event; a third ran south of Dunhuang, China ([[earthquakes]]).

## Depth profile

- Most events were shallow (≈10 km), but the feed also recorded deep-focus earthquakes,
  the deepest a 581 km event east of Levuka, Fiji — the deep Tonga–Fiji slab continues to
  register activity well below the crust ([[earthquakes]]).

## Reading the week

- The week's pattern is unremarkable for the western Pacific "Ring of Fire": high event
  counts concentrated on the Philippine and Indonesian subduction arcs, a handful of M6+
  events, and no recorded shock above M7. The data describes ordinary, elevated background
  seismicity along the world's most active plate boundaries ([[earthquakes]]).
