# Geophysical Weekly

> Synthesised from the week's L1 source notes under `../01 Sources/`. The `azimuth-curator`
> fleet role evolves this single note in place each week. azimuth reports **observed**
> seismicity from the USGS feed — what was recorded, never what will happen. Every claim
> links to the L1 note it rests on. (This cycle reads the live `2026-06-26` ingest, covering
> the seven days to 2026-06-26.)

## This week at a glance

- The USGS feed recorded 98 magnitude-4.5-or-greater earthquakes worldwide over the seven
  days to 2026-06-26, of which 30 were M5+ and 3 reached M6+ — broadly flat on the prior week
  (101), with the M6+ count falling from five to three as last week's Kamchatka pair rolled out
  of the rolling window, but the two **M7-or-greater** events still inside it ([[earthquakes]]).
- The headline of the window is a shallow doublet off **Yumare, Venezuela** — an M7.5 and an
  M7.2 struck within a minute of each other on 2026-06-24 (~23–28 km SE of Yumare, depths 10
  and 20 km) — the strongest events azimuth has recorded since the brief began, and notably
  **off** the usual western-Pacific arcs, on the South-American/Caribbean plate margin
  ([[earthquakes]]).
- A separate M6.9 struck 30 km ENE of Kuji, Japan the same day (2026-06-24, 51 km deep), while
  the western-Pacific subduction arcs again led the count — the Philippines (11), Indonesia
  (10) and Kamchatka/Kuril Russia (9) on top, with Japan (8), Tonga (6) and Papua New Guinea
  (5) behind ([[earthquakes]]).

## Largest events

- Three M6+ events fell in the seven-day window, two of them above M7: the M7.5 and M7.2 Yumare,
  Venezuela doublet (both 2026-06-24, shallow) and an M6.9 ENE of Kuji, Japan (2026-06-24,
  51 km). Last week's trailing-edge Kamchatka pair (M6.6 / M6.0 off Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky,
  2026-06-19) has now rolled out of the rolling window; the strongest remaining Kamchatka
  activity is an M5.8 pair ~140–171 km ESE of the city ([[earthquakes]]).
- The Venezuela doublet is the clearest feature of the week: two great-to-major shallow shocks
  seconds apart on a continental near-coast margin — a different tectonic setting from the
  oceanic subduction zones that dominate most weeks, and the only M7+ activity in the window
  ([[earthquakes]]).

## Where the activity clustered

- The standing high-count arcs held: the Philippines (a Catuday/Luzon cluster of repeated
  M4.5–M5.0 events plus the Mindanao margin), Indonesia (Tobelo/Halmahera) and the
  Kamchatka/Kuril margin off eastern Russia each logged 9–11 events; Japan rose to eight on the
  Kuji M6.9 sequence and a separate M5.8 at Yokoshiba ([[earthquakes]]).
- The Kamchatka offshore sequence that dominated two weeks ago has now fully cleared the rolling
  window; this week's strong activity is the Venezuela doublet and the Kuji, Japan M6.9, while a
  Tonga–Kermadec string, a Greece (Kastrí/Crete) cluster of M4.9–M5.2 events and an isolated
  M5.59 in Redwood Valley, California also registered ([[earthquakes]]).

## Depth profile

- Most events were shallow (≈10 km), including the Venezuela doublet, but the feed again
  recorded deep-focus earthquakes, the deepest an M4.8 at 606 km in the Fiji region — the deep
  Tonga–Fiji slab continues to register activity well below the crust, alongside
  intermediate-depth events (~200–520 km) under the Fiji–Kermadec arc ([[earthquakes]]).

## Reading the week

- The week's count was typical for the world's active plate boundaries — Philippine, Indonesian
  and Kamchatka arcs leading — and broadly flat on the prior week (98 vs 101). The strong-event
  picture, though, thinned at the top: the M6+ count fell from five to three as last week's
  Kamchatka M6.6/M6.0 pair aged out of the rolling window, leaving the released-energy story to
  the three 2026-06-24 events — the Yumare, Venezuela M7.5/M7.2 doublet and the Kuji, Japan M6.9,
  the only M6+ shocks still inside the window. azimuth records what the instruments measured —
  magnitude, location, depth, time — and stops there ([[earthquakes]]).
